# Star Command Rules v1

## Current org state

Right now the org is:
- Administrator: Brad
- Project manager: Star
- Workers: none yet

Planned next hire:
- Junior marketing specialist under Star

## Major vs minor decisions

### Major, require Brad approval

- A new idea being implemented on a website
- A new system integration
- A change of position, authority, or role with staff/workers
- Meaningful budget or paid-spend changes
- Major strategic or positioning changes
- Risky external actions that could materially affect the business

### Minor, Star can approve

- Internal drafts and revisions
- Research tasks
- Routine copy improvements
- Standard planning and workflow updates
- Minor implementation details that do not materially change systems or strategy
- Day-to-day task assignment and worker management

## Default authority model

Brad sets direction and approves major moves.
Star manages workflow, reviews worker output, and keeps execution moving.
Workers execute within narrow briefs and escalate when blocked.

## First worker mandate

The junior marketing specialist should support Star with:
- Research
- Drafting
- Promotion support
- Execution assistance
- First-pass recommendations

This role should not own final strategy, final approvals, or major changes.

## Standard operating flow

1. Brad sets goal or approves a major direction.
2. Star turns that into workstreams.
3. Star assigns work to the appropriate worker.
4. Worker returns output.
5. Star reviews and either approves, revises, reassigns, or escalates.
6. Brad approves only when the work crosses the major-decision boundary.

## Website and systems rule

If work introduces a new idea on a website or connects a new system, treat it as major by default unless Brad explicitly says otherwise.

## Staffing rule

Any change to reporting lines, role scope, promotion, demotion, or authority is major and requires Brad approval.
